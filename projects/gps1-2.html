What is paired programing?

Pair programming is a collaborative effort between two programmers working to solve the same problem. Pair programming entails a balance between research and execution on behalf of both programmers to achieve the desired objective.

Why is paired programing important to DBC?

Paired programming is important because not only are you learning together but it is a very efficient way of solving problems because mistakes are more easily realized and both members of the team can concentrate on specific tasks while still keeping track of each others work.
